# Loom Script — Dataleap Quickstart Hub Demo

**Target length: 3-4 minutes**
**Tone: Confident, casual, energetic — like you're talking to a co-founder, not pitching a VC**

---

## Opening (15 seconds)

"Hey Jan — I'm Niran. I'm applying for the Chief of Staff role, and instead of just sending a resume, I wanted to show you what I'd actually do on the job. I built three things."

## The Problem (30 seconds)

"I signed up for Dataleap and the first thing I noticed is that new users face a blank canvas problem — they know AI agents are powerful, but they don't know what to automate first. This is the same activation challenge every flexible tool faces. Notion solved it with templates. Zapier solved it with their template directory, which now drives 8.5 million monthly visitors. Nobody in the AI agent space has built a real SEO-driven version of this yet."

## Demo: Quickstart Hub (60 seconds)

*Screen share the deployed Vercel app — have the landing page open*

"So I built this — the Dataleap Quickstart Hub. It's 25 curated agent templates organized by role."

*Click through the role filter — show Sales, then Engineering*

"You can filter by Sales, Ops, Engineering — each card shows time saved, setup time, and the tools required."

*Click into the Pre-Call Brief Generator (or Daily Briefing Email)*

"The key differentiator is this — every template has complete, copy-paste-ready agent instructions. A user can literally grab these, paste them into Dataleap, and have a working agent in minutes."

*Navigate to /advisor, type a quick message*

"And for users who don't know where to start — there's an AI-powered advisor. It asks about your role and pain points, then recommends specific templates with personalized ROI estimates. No competitor has a standalone tool like this."

## Demo: Dataleap Agents (45 seconds)

*Switch to your Dataleap tab showing your agents*

"I also built three agents directly on the platform to prove these templates actually work. This is a competitor intel digest that monitors Lindy, Relevance, and Bardeen weekly."

*Show the email output from the competitor intel run*

"Here's the actual output from a real run — [say one specific thing it caught, e.g., 'it picked up Bardeen's new pricing tier and flagged what that means for Dataleap's positioning']. I also built a content repurposer that turns blog posts into LinkedIn, Twitter, and email content, and an onboarding advisor that complements your existing onboarding by connecting new users to the template gallery. Each one took about 20 minutes to build — which is exactly the story we want every new user to experience."

## Strategy (40 seconds)

"All of this connects to a bigger growth thesis. Dataleap's number one lever right now is reducing time to first agent. The template gallery drives organic discovery through SEO — the same playbook that built Zapier's top of funnel. The AI advisor converts curious visitors into activated users. And community templates down the road create a flywheel where users attract more users."

"I wrote up a full growth playbook with six prioritized experiments — I'll link it below."

## Close (15 seconds)

"I built all of this in a few hours because I wanted to show you how I work — fast, autonomous, and focused on what actually moves the needle. I'm ready to do this full-time and help Dataleap become the default way teams work with AI."

---

## Before You Hit Record

- Have two browser tabs ready: (1) Vercel app on the landing page, (2) Dataleap dashboard showing your agents
- Close all other tabs and clean your desktop — no clutter
- Review your agent run outputs and pick one specific detail to call out
- If the AI Advisor is live (API key set), have a quick demo conversation ready to show
- Test that the template filters and detail page copy button work

## While Recording

- Speak at a natural pace — don't rush, but don't drag
- Smile on the opener and closer
- If you stumble, keep going — scrappy > polished for a startup
- One take if possible — it shows confidence
- Show, don't tell — click through things while you talk, don't just describe them
